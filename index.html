Who We Are

About RRWO

Rasira Rwanda Women Organization is a victim-led, grassroots women's organization established in 2023, based in Burera District, Northern Province of Rwanda.

🌱 Our Story

RRWO was founded in 2023 by women survivors who understood that true healing comes from within the community. Born from shared experience, RRWO exists to ensure no woman in Burera District faces violence, poverty, or injustice alone. We are victim-led — meaning our leadership and direction come from those with lived experience.

🎯 Our Mission

To empower women and girls in Burera District through comprehensive support programs that address psychosocial healing, economic independence, legal protection, and community solidarity — building a Rwanda where every woman can live with dignity and safety.

👁️ Our Vision

A Northern Rwanda where all women — regardless of background, identity, or circumstance — have equal access to opportunities, safety, justice, and community belonging.

💎 Our Values

Survivor-Led
Those with lived experience guide our work and decisions.
Dignity
Every woman deserves respect, care, and equal treatment.
Solidarity
We grow stronger together, supporting each other at all levels.
Accountability
Transparent use of resources and honest community reporting.
Inclusion
Welcoming women of all backgrounds, including LBTGNC persons.
Community First
All decisions are guided by what benefits the community most.

📍 Where We Work

RRWO is based in and primarily serves Burera District, located in the Northern Province of Rwanda.

Burera District Northern Province Rwanda

🎯 Who We Serve

  • Women and girls who are survivors of gender-based violence
  • Economically vulnerable women in Burera District
  • LBTGNC persons facing discrimination and exclusion
  • Women-headed households in need of legal or psychosocial support
  • Community members seeking to support women's rights and empowerment

Our Work

Activities &
Programs

From community outreach to psychosocial support — see how RRWO is transforming lives in Burera District every day.

🤝
Community Outreach & Mobilization
Ongoing · Burera District
RRWO regularly conducts community outreach visits across Burera District to identify vulnerable women, raise awareness about available support services, and build solidarity networks. Our outreach teams go door-to-door and village-to-village, ensuring women in remote areas are not left behind.
Door-to-door visits Village meetings Awareness raising
💰
Resource Mobilization
Ongoing · Community-funded
We mobilize resources from within the community — encouraging those who have more to support those who have less. This includes organizing contributions from community members, reaching out to partners, and coordinating material support such as food, farming tools, and basic household items for the most vulnerable families.
Community fundraising In-kind donations Partner outreach
🌾
Agricultural Support & Cooperative Work
Active · Field-based
RRWO members come together to support women-headed households with farming through collective work (ubudehe spirit). Women support each other on each other's fields, share tools, and exchange knowledge — turning agriculture into a source of solidarity as well as livelihood.
Collective farming Tool sharing Food security
💚
Psychosocial & Emotional Support
Ongoing · Confidential
For women who have experienced violence, trauma, or loss, RRWO provides a safe space for sharing, healing, and support. We connect women with available counseling services and facilitate peer support groups where women can speak freely and without judgment.
Safe spaces Peer support Counseling referrals
⚖️
Legal Rights Awareness
Planned · In development
RRWO is developing a program to help women understand their legal rights — around land, inheritance, domestic violence protection, and custody. We aim to connect women with legal aid organizations and support them in navigating formal systems.
Rights education Legal referrals Documentation support
